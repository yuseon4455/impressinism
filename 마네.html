Édouard Manet

1832.1.23 ~ 1883.4.30

He is called the father of impressionism. As possessing a sophisticated urban sensibility, he was an unprecedented painter in his penmanship, keenly capturing the lively reality of his surroundings. The achievement is great in that it performed an intermediary role linking tradition and innovation, such as introducing light to the conventional dark screen.

Le Déjeuner sur l'herbe

The painting depicts a nude woman with two well-dressed men in a rural setting and another woman almost naked.

Langpi

Olympia, presented to the Paris Chalons in 1865, was criticized. Conservatives in particular criticized the work as "obscene and vulgar".

Folies-Bergère’s Bar

This painting is believed to be Edouard Manet's last major work. It depicts a scene from the Folies-Bergere nightclub in Paris. The painting originally belonged to Manet's close friend, composer Emmanuel Chabrier.
