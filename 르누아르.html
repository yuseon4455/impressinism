Auguste Renoir

1841.2.25 ~ 1919.12.0 3

After a trip to Italy, he drew lines and forms clearly with light tones, and painted works with a classical tendency, which gave deep meaning to the composition of the screen. After that, he broke away from Impressionism and regained his own rich color expression, establishing a mature style by contrasting primary colors.

Luncheon of the Boating Party

It depicts Renoir's friends resting on the balcony of the Maison Fournaise restaurant on the banks of the Seine in Chateau, France. It shows the rich form, the fluidity of the brushstrokes and the flickering light.

Dance at Le Moulin de la Galette

One of the most famous masterpieces of the Impressionists. This painting depicts a typical Sunday afternoon at the original Moulin de la Galette in the Montmartre district of Paris.

La Grenouillère

In the early days of Impressionism, it was painted by two poor friends and a fellow artist at the same time as Claude Monet's Bain à la Grenouillère.It shows the "camembert", a small island planted with a single tree.
