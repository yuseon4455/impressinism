Edgar Degas

1834.7.19 ~ 1917.9.27

He is a famous French painter and sculptor. Although Degas is regarded as one of the founders of Impressionism, he preferred to be called a realist and did not paint outdoors, as many Impressionist painters did.

Dans un Café

When it was first shown in 1876, the painting was criticized by critics for being ugly and disgusting. It was kept until in 1892, but again became an object of ridicule. Other early titles were A sketch of French Café and Figures at Café. When it was exhibited in London in 1893, the title was changed to L'Absinthe.

The Rehearsal

It is said that he shows his talent as he finds a subject in the modern life of Paris. Not only can it be seen, but it also has literary value. A moment that can be seen in ballet practice time is captured in a single picture.

The Dancing Class

This is the first of Degas' "Ballet Paintings". This painting depicts a dance class at the Paris Opera. The dancer in the center is Josephine Gozellin.
