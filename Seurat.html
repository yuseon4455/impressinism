Georges Pierre Seurat

1859.12.2 ~ 1891.3.29

French painter and founder of Neo-Impressionism. His most famous masterpiece, Sunday Afternoon on the Island of Grande Jatte, turned the direction of modern art with the onset of Neo-Impressionism and became a symbol of 19th-century painting.

A Sunday on La Grande Jatte

Georges Seurat depicted people relaxing in a suburban park on an island in the Seine River called La Grande Jatte. He later added small dots, also in complementary colors, that appear as solid and luminous forms when seen from a distance.

The Eiffel Tower

Seurat painted this painting in defense of the Eiffel Tower a month before the Eiffel Tower was completed. It was painted a month before it was completed, so the top of the tower is painted in an unfinished state.

The Seine at Courbevoie

Soft green and gray-blue tones are brought to life through a touch of bright yellow or pink. The warm, orange-brown of the panel, visible underneath the layers of paint, also plays a role in the composition.
