Eugène Henri Paul Gauguin

1848.6.7 ~ 1903.5.8

Gauguin's work inspired French avant-garde writers such as Pablo Picasso and Henri Matisse. After Gauguin's death, Ambroise Bollard, an art dealer in Paris, held two posthumous exhibitions in Paris, and only then did he gain fame.

Arearea

He took his inspiration for imaginary scenes in his paintings from what he saw around him, as well as from local stories and ancient religious traditions. Arearea is representative of these works where dream and reality coexist.

Je vous salue Marie

Gauguin's only religious painting, depicting the Virgin and Child as a native of Tahiti. It is also evaluated that the typical Western primitivism view of the time that women and nature were identified as the combination of a woman with an exposed upper body and the richness of nature appears.

Femmes de Tahiti

It depicts a flat color surface, and with this effect, the two women stand out greatly. In addition, it shows simple figures, strong outlines, and expressions of symbolic elements that seem to have been influenced by primitive and Japanese art.
